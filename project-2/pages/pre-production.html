Pre-Production

Clean Your Workspace

At some point during this project you are going to have one hundred or so individual drawings sopping wet with a watercolor wash, You are going to need space, and lots of it, preferably someplace where you can stand up while you are working. Dirt, grime, pizza sauce, whatever it is marring your work surface has got to go. I recommend a coarse sponge and white cider vinegar.

Gather Your Materials

I've split the materials lists into three so that all three phases of the process are separate.

  1. Pre-Production

    • Counter space
    • White cider vinegar
    • A coarse sponge

  2. Production

    • 100 or more 3x5 index cards, spiral bound for convenience
    • A Pencil
    • Ink pens of all sorts
    • Brush pens
    • Crayons
    • Colored Pencils
    • Watercolor Paint
    • Watercolor Brushes
    • Paper Towels
    • Bowls, cups, anything that holds water
    • A Lightboard or Light box

  3. Post-Production

    • A Scanner
    • Adobe Premiere
    • A Youtube Account

Practice your "Hand"

This is the point at which you draw until you are no longer aware of your drawings. Draw until you no longer see what you have made. Draw until your hands instinctively seek out a blank page and you have no concern for the last thing you drew. The last thing you drew is unimportant, what is important is the next page, and the next page and so on until you are like an animation version of John Henry, pounding away at an impossible task despite the onset of automation, become a machine, thoughtlessly driving the pistons of your drawing hand forward until you can see the light of day underneath that mountain. Remember how replaceable you are and let that be your fuel.
